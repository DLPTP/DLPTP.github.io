Digitalized language proficiency testing & practice

LEAD (Language & Education) offers Computer-Assisted digitalized language proficiency testing & practice systems for different languages. DLPTP stsyems include digital exercises with individualised feedback, complexity analysis, digital placement tests (C-test format), etc. These systems are designed for higher education.

C-Test Editor

The C-test format - based on the Reduced Redundancy Testing Principle â€“ enables teachers and researchers to measure global second language (L2) proficiency. It was developed in the early eighties 1980s (for English and German) on the basis of the Cloze test. Meanwhile Since then, the C-test has become one of the most widely used placement test formats in higher education, and with more than 500 publications on C-tests for many languages, it is also one of the most researched test instruments in language testing (including L1, L2 and Hheritage Llanguages). According to the so-called â€˜Rule of 2-2-2' it consists of 4 to 6 short texts (with 20 to 25 gaps, respectively) in which starting from the second sentence the second half of every second word is gapped has to be completed (with words consisting of an odd number of letters, half-plus-1 has to be completedis gapped); the last sentence is also left intact. In C-tests, examinees are required to add the gapped part of the words (i.e., reconstruct the original words) based on general language knowledge and context knowledge (including grammar, morphology, semantics, and syntax). C-tests are increasingly administered automatically (gapping, administering, scoring, and feedback to the teacher). Finally, C-tests have a high validity besides their favorable cost- benefit ratio.

RUSSIAN

In teaching Russian as a second language, there is the need for a tool which can automatically generate a valid digital placement test for learners. The Russian extension of the existing C-test editor is currently being developed in collaboration with LEAD and the Department of Slavic Studies as part of a Master thesis in Computational Linguistics. Along with the C-test itself, the tool will also provide users with some basic complexity and readability measures such as the Flesch Reading Ease and various type-token ratios, as well as a linguistic feature analysis based on the TORFL (Test of Russian as a Foreign Language) standards in order to help determine the difficulty level of a text and its appropriateness for learners at various proficiency levels.

ARABIC

Learning Arabic as a second language requires effective assessment digital tools. Arabic is being extended to C-test editor to develop an automatic C-test generation for Arabic assessment. This project is a part of Master thesis in Computational Linguistics in collaboration with LEAD, Language Center (Fachsprachenzentrum), Center for Islamic Theology (ZITh), and the Department of Oriental and Islamic Studies at the University of Tübingen . Additionally, an Arabic group is formed for C-test development that is consisting of academics from other universities. The extension of Arabic provides users with language-specific features. First, the issue of the text direction was solved, and it is currently possible to use any Arabic text without struggling to fix the format or punctation positions. The user is also able to choose whether they want to enable or disable diacritics. Diacritics are automatically added to the Arabic text or removed. Some other linguistic challenges include the definite article (Ø§Ù„), as it is always connected to the word as a prefix, plus as well as some prepositions. Theseis issues may lead to invalid gapping because the definite article or/and the prepositions can be as long as half of the word, which results in gapping of the actual lexical item. This problem was also solved by giving the user the option to exclude the definite article from gapping. It is known that Arabic is a highly inflectional language, so there is a section in the C-test Eeditor which shows the stem of each gapped word. Finally, extra linguistic features were also extended into the program, such as counting the number of verbs, adjectives, relative pronouns, etc. To measure the difficulty of the text, linguistic complexity and readability metrics are also included in the program.

CHINESE

As one of the most learned languages in the world, few works are done in C-tests for Mandarin Chinese, while a need for an effective Chinese proficiency testing tool is growing. An automatic Chinese C-test takes several language-specific properties into account and provides multiple options when it comes to the test design. For instance, since the language can be written in either simplified or traditional characters, the C-test editor offers a choice of these two kinds of writing in the testletsone can choose the testlets in a C-test out of these two kinds of writing. Depending on the difficulty level, all the testlets can be shown with or without Pinyin. Similarly, scoring offers user-specific options. After tests are graded, feedbacks will be given, along with some tips to further improve language learning.

Future Perspective

Tübingen University is developing concepts for C-tests in languages for which C-tests are not yet in use or for which a concept has neither been created yet nor much investigated into. Furthermore, Tübingen University is developing a system to combine the C-test editor with other tools in order to offer a multifunctional system which will include linguistic complexity analyzers, automated and scaffolding feedback, modules for corpus management such as feature selection, etc. - for different languages, including Arabic.
